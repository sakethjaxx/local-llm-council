# Hiring Decision: Staff Engineer vs Two Mid-Level Engineers

We have $280K/year to spend on engineering headcount. Advise on the right hire.

## Company Context

- 12-person startup, Series A ($4.2M raised)
- Product: B2B data pipeline tool (self-hosted + SaaS)
- Current eng team: 3 engineers (1 senior, 2 mid-level), 1 EM
- Runway: 22 months
- Biggest technical debt: no observability, no CI/CD, monolith needs splitting

## Option A: One Staff Engineer ($270K total comp)

- 10+ years experience
- Has led platform teams at two mid-size companies
- Strong distributed systems background
- Interview signal: excellent systems design, average coding speed
- Concern: may get bored without a team to lead

## Option B: Two Mid-Level Engineers ($130K + $140K = $270K total)

- Candidate 1: 4 years exp, strong Python/data background, shipped 3 pipeline products
- Candidate 2: 3 years exp, frontend-leaning, React + some Go, fast learner
- Both passed technical screens
- Concern: no one to mentor them — senior engineer is already stretched

## What We Need In The Next 12 Months

1. Split monolith into at least 2 services (3 months)
2. Ship observability layer (Prometheus + Grafana) (2 months)
3. Build CI/CD pipeline from scratch (1 month)
4. Deliver 3 major product features (9 months)

## The Real Trade-Off

Depth vs. throughput. One person who can architect and set standards, or two people who can ship features faster.

## Council Question

Which hire? What's the risk of each? Is there a third path?
